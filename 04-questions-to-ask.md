# Questions To Ask Sandvik

Good questions show that you understand service ownership as business value, not only IT delivery.

## Top Questions For Riitta

1. How do you define the Customer Portal service boundaries today? Which applications, suppliers, and integrations are included in the Service Owner scope?

2. What are the most important outcomes you want this Service Owner to deliver in the first 6 to 12 months?

3. What are the current biggest pain points for My Sandvik from a customer, business, or IT perspective?

4. How is the portal roadmap currently governed across Sales and Marketing, Parts and Services, Digital Mining, IT, and regional stakeholders?

5. Which KPIs matter most today: availability, incidents, adoption, quote/order conversion, warranty cycle time, customer satisfaction, supplier performance, or something else?

6. How is supplier performance managed today, and where would you like the Service Owner to strengthen vendor governance?

7. What is the current operating model between Stockholm, Tampere, global IT, business teams, and external suppliers?

8. How do security, application standards, and compliance requirements enter the roadmap and release process today?

9. How mature is the current service reporting model? Is there already a dashboard for SLAs, incidents, roadmap progress, budget, and risks?

10. What would make you say after one year that hiring this person was a success?

## Questions For Lotta Or HR

1. What are the next steps after this interview, and what timeline should I expect?

2. Is there likely to be a technical or stakeholder interview after this competency interview?

3. How does Sandvik support onboarding for someone joining a global IT and business-facing role?

4. How does Sandvik encourage development for people working across IT, service ownership, and digital transformation?

5. What is the expected office rhythm for Stockholm World Trade Center versus remote collaboration?

## Closing Question

Use this near the end:

"Based on our conversation, is there any area of my background you would like me to clarify further, especially around service ownership, supplier coordination, budget involvement, or customer-facing digital platforms?"

This gives you a chance to address concerns before the interview ends.

## Questions To Avoid In The First Interview

Avoid leading with:
- Detailed vacation or benefits questions before they raise compensation.
- Criticism of the portal without internal context.
- Questions that imply you want a purely project management role.
- Overly technical architecture questions before you understand service boundaries.
