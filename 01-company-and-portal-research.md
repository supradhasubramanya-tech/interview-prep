# Sandvik And My Sandvik Research

Research snapshot prepared on May 12, 2026 for the IT Service Owner - Customer Portal interview.

## Sandvik In One Minute

Sandvik is a Swedish global industrial technology group headquartered in Stockholm. The company provides products, services, equipment, tools, and digital solutions for mining, manufacturing, and infrastructure. In the 2025 Annual Report, Sandvik describes itself as focused on improving customers' productivity and being at the forefront of digitalization.

Useful facts to remember:
- Purpose: "Advancing the world through engineering."
- Core values: Winning together, Curiosity, Responsibility, Customer focus.
- 2026 strategy period: "Advancing to 2030."
- Strategic objectives: drive growth, empower high-performing teams, accelerate digital, deliver profitability and efficiency, lead industry innovation.
- Digital target: SEK 13 billion in digital revenue by 2030.
- Culture language: collaboration, inclusion, safety-first responsibility, innovation, customer value.

Interview angle:
Connect your answer to customer productivity, reliability, governance, and measurable digital value. Sandvik will likely appreciate language that balances innovation with operational discipline.

## Sandvik Mining Context

Business area Mining is a global supplier of equipment, tools, parts, services, digital solutions, and sustainability-driving technologies for mining and infrastructure.

2025 Annual Report facts:
- Revenues: SEK 62,971 million.
- Adjusted EBITA: SEK 13,045 million.
- Adjusted EBITA margin: 20.7 percent.
- Employees: 18,395 full-time equivalent.
- Strategic priorities: underground leadership, aftermarket growth, surface drilling, automation and mining software and technology, electrification.
- Market position: underground trucks and loaders, drill rigs, surface drilling, mechanical cutting, aftermarket, ground support, rock tools, and end-to-end digital solutions.
- Competitors mentioned by Sandvik: Epiroc, Caterpillar, Komatsu Mining, and others depending on niche.

Interview angle:
The Customer Portal is close to strategic priorities because it supports aftermarket, parts, fleet information, digital services, customer self-service, recurring digital offerings, and service reliability.

## My Sandvik Customer Portal

Public Sandvik pages describe My Sandvik as the digital customer interface for customers with Sandvik equipment. It enables customers to purchase products, track fleet data, and manage business directly online.

Publicly described features:
- Shop: request quotes and purchase more than 130,000 products online.
- My fleet: see fleet overview, location, status, warranty dates, and related information.
- Electronic manuals: access interactive parts, technical, operational, and maintenance manuals.
- Bulletins: receive new and historical safety, parts, and critical information.
- Digital Service Solutions: access reporting and insights on connected machines in real time.
- Warranty portal: create and follow up warranty claims for the fleet.
- Quick sign-up and login.

Additional public detail from Sandvik's 2020 portal release:
- My Sandvik has been described as an industry-leading customer portal since 2017.
- It provides a 360-degree view of the customer's fleet.
- The electronic parts catalog helps identify the right spare part using diagrams, price and availability checks, quote requests, and ordering.
- Fleet data monitoring gives access to My Sandvik Insight and My Sandvik Productivity reports.
- It is positioned as globally available 24/7.

## Why My Sandvik Matters

For customers:
- Reduces time spent searching for parts, manuals, warranty information, and service data.
- Improves uptime by helping customers find correct parts and make faster maintenance decisions.
- Gives mine/site teams better visibility into fleet status, machine health, safety alerts, and utilization.
- Supports procurement, maintenance, and service workflows in one place.

For Sandvik:
- Strengthens aftermarket growth through easier parts and service access.
- Increases digital engagement with customers.
- Makes customer data, fleet data, and service data more usable.
- Supports recurring digital service offerings such as Insight, Productivity, telemetry, and remote monitoring.
- Provides a direct channel for customer experience, adoption, and measurable value.

## Likely Portal Users

This is an inference from the public feature set, not a direct Sandvik source.

Likely external personas:
- Maintenance planner who needs manuals, parts catalogs, and bulletins.
- Procurement or parts buyer who needs price, availability, quotes, and ordering.
- Service manager who needs warranty dates, claims, and fleet service visibility.
- Site or shift manager who needs utilization, productivity, alerts, and operational dashboards.
- Equipment owner or fleet manager who needs the full fleet overview.

Likely internal personas:
- Sales and marketing stakeholders who own the customer journey and commercial experience.
- Parts and services teams responsible for aftermarket business.
- Digital mining and telemetry teams responsible for connected fleet data.
- Customer support and service teams handling incidents, claims, and questions.
- IT teams responsible for application lifecycle, security, integrations, identity, release management, and suppliers.

## Service Owner Priorities For This Portal

For this role, speak like someone who thinks across business value, operations, and technology.

High-priority themes:
- Reliability: availability, incident response, problem management, performance, operational readiness.
- User experience: seamless login, clear navigation, fast quote/order flows, easy manuals and warranty journeys.
- Governance: service ownership model, roadmap intake, decision forums, application standards, security compliance.
- Stakeholder alignment: Sales, Marketing, Parts and Services, Digital Mining, IT, suppliers, support, and regional business teams.
- Supplier management: vendor performance, contracts, SLAs, delivery commitments, escalations, cost transparency.
- Data and integrations: product master data, equipment/fleet data, warranty data, manuals/content, quote/order systems, telemetry reporting.
- Continuous improvement: service analytics, customer feedback, backlog prioritization, root cause trends, release learning loops.
- Security: identity and access management, customer data protection, secure supplier delivery, vulnerability follow-up, audit readiness.

## Metrics To Mention

Possible KPIs for a Customer Portal IT service:
- Availability and uptime against SLA.
- Incident volume, severity, mean time to restore, and repeat incidents.
- Login success rate and access request cycle time.
- Quote/order completion rate and drop-off points.
- Search success for parts/manuals.
- Manual or bulletin content freshness.
- Warranty claim cycle time.
- Active users, active customer accounts, and adoption by region.
- Customer satisfaction or support ticket sentiment.
- Supplier SLA adherence and release predictability.
- Security vulnerability aging and compliance status.
- Portal performance, API latency, and data freshness.

## Smart Interview Framing

Use this phrasing:

"My Sandvik is a business-critical digital service, not only an application. I would want to understand the service boundaries, customer journeys, suppliers, integrations, SLA model, current pain points, and the roadmap. Then I would make the service measurable and create a governance rhythm that helps business and IT make clear prioritization decisions."
