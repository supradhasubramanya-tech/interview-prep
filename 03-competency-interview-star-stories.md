# Competency Interview STAR Stories

Sandvik says the interview will be competency based. Prepare stories using STAR:

- Situation: the context.
- Task: your responsibility.
- Action: what you personally did.
- Result: measurable outcome and learning.

Keep each answer to about 2 minutes. The goal is not to recite the script, but to rehearse the structure.

## 1. Improving A Service

Likely question:
"Tell us about a time you improved an IT service or process."

Story:
- Situation: At Qwikcilver/Pine Labs, service requests and customer-facing delivery items were moving slowly across multiple teams.
- Task: Improve flow, visibility, and turnaround without losing control over priorities and escalations.
- Action: Introduced Kanban-based workflows, WIP limits, visual management, clearer escalation paths, and regular service review touchpoints.
- Result: Reduced service request turnaround time by 50 percent.
- Sandvik connection: Shows continuous improvement, service thinking, SLA visibility, and user value.

Key phrase:
"I did not treat it only as a team productivity issue. I treated it as a service flow issue: visibility, ownership, escalation, and measurable improvement."

## 2. Leading Cross-Functional Delivery

Likely question:
"Describe a complex delivery where you had to coordinate many stakeholders."

Story:
- Situation: Enterprise payment platform delivery involved issuing-adjacent products, gift cards, payment gateways, integrations, onboarding, and operations.
- Task: Coordinate Product, Engineering, Security, Operations, QA, vendors, customers, and leadership.
- Action: Created governance forums, release plans, dashboards, risk/dependency tracking, and customer review rhythms. Negotiated scope and aligned releases to customer priorities.
- Result: Improved delivery performance by 40 percent and strengthened transparency across 80+ engineers and stakeholder groups.
- Sandvik connection: Similar to coordinating Customer Portal stakeholders, internal teams, and suppliers.

Key phrase:
"My role was to create shared visibility so stakeholders could make decisions based on facts rather than assumptions."

## 3. Customer-Facing Service Ownership

Likely question:
"Tell us about a time you acted as the main point of contact for a customer-facing platform."

Story:
- Situation: At Qwikcilver, customers needed onboarding, integrations, issue resolution, SLA tracking, and service performance visibility.
- Task: Be the interface between customers and internal teams while maintaining delivery and service accountability.
- Action: Ran customer reviews, managed escalations, translated issues into backlog items, coordinated technical teams and vendors, and reported service performance.
- Result: Improved customer confidence, increased delivery predictability, and supported smoother onboarding and integration delivery.
- Sandvik connection: Directly maps to being the main point of contact for My Sandvik portal-related IT topics.

Key phrase:
"I try to make customers feel that there is clear ownership, even when many teams are involved behind the scenes."

## 4. Governance And Compliance

Likely question:
"Describe a time you handled governance, compliance, or audit readiness."

Story:
- Situation: Payment platform work required structured SDLC documentation, process controls, and audit readiness.
- Task: Support governance reporting and audit presentation responsibilities.
- Action: Maintained documentation, coordinated inputs from teams, made process evidence visible, supported first-party ISO audit presentation responsibilities, and tracked risks and dependencies.
- Result: Strengthened audit readiness and made governance part of normal delivery reporting.
- Sandvik connection: Portal service ownership includes security, application standards, compliance, and supplier governance.

Key phrase:
"My approach is to make compliance operational, not ceremonial. The evidence should be visible in normal service and delivery routines."

## 5. Agile Application Delivery

Likely question:
"How have you used agile ways of working in application management or product delivery?"

Story:
- Situation: At Philips, Forecast2Plan transformation required product ownership and sprint execution across offshore and on-site teams.
- Task: Translate business needs into epics, features, user stories, acceptance criteria, KPIs, and delivery plans.
- Action: Prioritized the backlog, facilitated sprint execution, managed risks and dependencies, and created executive dashboards.
- Result: Improved transparency and alignment between business needs and delivery execution.
- Sandvik connection: Role asks for application management and development in an agile setup.

Key phrase:
"Agile is useful when it improves value flow and stakeholder learning, not when it becomes a ceremony checklist."

## 6. Digital Adoption And Change

Likely question:
"Tell us about a digital transformation or adoption initiative."

Story:
- Situation: At Publicis Sapient, Microsoft 365 Copilot rollout required enterprise adoption, operational readiness, governance, and change communication.
- Task: Support rollout activities and align success metrics, governance, and user enablement.
- Action: Coordinated stakeholders, supported adoption planning, helped define readiness activities, and connected business usage with operational considerations.
- Result: Built experience in digital adoption, governance, and measurable success criteria for enterprise users.
- Sandvik connection: My Sandvik portal value depends not only on functionality, but on adoption, trust, and customer experience.

Key phrase:
"A portal succeeds when users trust it enough to make it part of their daily workflow."

## 7. Handling Conflict Or Competing Priorities

Likely question:
"Tell us about a time stakeholders had conflicting priorities. What did you do?"

Story:
- Situation: In enterprise platform delivery, customer commitments, engineering capacity, security constraints, and release timelines often competed.
- Task: Create a prioritization approach that protected delivery value and service reliability.
- Action: Made trade-offs visible, clarified business impact, negotiated scope, escalated when needed, and used governance forums to make decisions explicit.
- Result: Improved delivery performance by 40 percent and reduced ambiguity in release planning.
- Sandvik connection: Customer Portal roadmap alignment will require balancing business requests, supplier capacity, security, technical debt, and customer impact.

Key phrase:
"I do not try to remove conflict by smoothing it over. I make the trade-offs visible so the right decision can be made."

## 8. Leading Without Direct Authority

Likely question:
"How do you lead teams when people do not report to you?"

Story:
- Situation: In program and service delivery roles, teams were cross-functional and often not directly reporting to you.
- Task: Create alignment, accountability, and trust across Product, Engineering, Security, Operations, QA, vendors, and leadership.
- Action: Used clear working agreements, transparent dashboards, regular governance, clear escalation paths, and coaching.
- Result: Coordinated 80+ engineers in platform delivery and coached 60+ professionals across delivery practices.
- Sandvik connection: Service Owner roles often rely on influence, trust, and structured governance rather than line authority.

Key phrase:
"I lead through clarity, cadence, and trust: people need to know the goal, the decision path, and how their work connects to value."

## 9. Learning From A Challenge

Likely question:
"Tell us about a time something did not go as planned."

Prepare one honest example before the interview. Choose a story where:
- The issue was real but not catastrophic.
- You took ownership.
- You changed a process afterward.
- The learning is relevant to service ownership.

Suggested structure:
- Situation: A release, customer integration, or stakeholder expectation became misaligned.
- Task: Recover confidence and prevent recurrence.
- Action: Replanned with stakeholders, clarified acceptance criteria, improved risk visibility, and adjusted governance.
- Result: Delivery stabilized and the team improved release readiness or communication.
- Learning: Earlier alignment on scope, dependencies, and readiness criteria prevents late surprises.

Key phrase:
"The lesson for me was that transparency has to start before a risk becomes an escalation."
